§ v · about

The scope, in brief.

Four disciplines, two classes of work, one notebook.

There is thus the technical side where much of the content focuses on the junction of engineering, programming and mathematics. The motivation for this combination is that new multi-discipline career paths are emerging as technologies like LLMs are reducing the barrier to entry. The additional opportunities are creating more jobs and reducing competition. Furthermore, these three disciplines seldom seem to be considered complementary by people that practise it and the hope is to create more harmony in the community. There are naturally fields like science and business that link well to software, engineering and maths, but are left out of scope.

But there is also the human relational topic that is deeply integrated in engineering and software development. Technical excellence can be, and is very often, stifled due to detrimental suspicion or competition amongst colleagues (horizontal) and distrust between employees and leadership (vertical). Additionally, the growing lack of mentorship and elder figures nurturing young potential is leaving a wake of under-developed professionals. Technical excellence, sustainability and innovation go hand-in-hand with human relationships.

The heart (jobs, in terms of Clayton Christensen's language) is to:

  • Inspire — to show what is possible and present a path-to-entry to anyone interested. To make readers realise they can understand and implement something they might not think they could.
  • Empower — to encourage readers to embrace their talent set and follow their unique career path. To show how and where to start. To help organise and prioritize ideas. By explaining complex topics in incremental and clear visual ways.
  • Guide — to highlight the importance of leaders that can humbly encourage and empower successors. To create work environments of trust, respect and the freedom to develop people in their unique ways.
